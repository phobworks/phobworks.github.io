Maac House Incubator Program

Introducing MaaC House

MaaC House Ltd. is a creative and cultural powerhouse dedicated to nurturing African arts, culture, and talent. We aim to empower individuals and communities by providing platforms for creative expression, professional growth, and impactful storytelling.

Our Mission

To cultivate a thriving ecosystem for African creatives, bridging the gap between traditional heritage and global innovation.

Our Vision

To transform African narratives by empowering talents who will inspire, uplift, and lead future generations.

What is the MaaC House Incubator Program?

The MaaC House Incubator Program is a sustainable talent development initiative designed to provide emerging African creatives with the resources, mentorship, and opportunities to excel in their fields.

Key Objectives:

  • Provide comprehensive support for creative growth and business development.
  • Build a sustainable mentorship model to empower successive generations of talent.
  • Foster an ecosystem where African creativity thrives on a global scale.

The Model:

  • Phase 1: One creative is incubated annually.
  • Phase 2:Each creative graduates and mentors 3 other talents.
  • Phase 3:These mentees go on to mentor 3 other talents each, creating an exponential impact.

Meet Tama Nisa - The First Incubatee

Tama Nisa, the Founder and CEO of MaaC House Ltd., is also the first creative in the incubator program. A multi-talented artist, creative entrepreneur, and spiritual evolution activist, Tama embodies the essence of African resilience, innovation, and artistry.

    Progress So Far

  • Music: Tama is developing a 15-track album, Rise of Selepower, working with the renowned producer Irock Classic. Five tracks have been completed during her music production camp.
  • Impact: Tama's work goes beyond music; her themes of empowerment and cultural pride inspire global audiences.
  • Mentorship: Tama is poised to mentor 3 other creatives, fostering a ripple effect of talent development.

Why Tama Nisa?

Her journey is a testament to the transformative power of the incubator program. She represents the untapped potential of African creatives when given the right resources and support.
